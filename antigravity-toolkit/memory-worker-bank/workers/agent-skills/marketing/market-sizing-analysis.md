This skill provides a structured approach to quantifying market potential, crucial for any aspiring entrepreneur or business analyst. It goes beyond simple estimations, guiding users through sophisticated methodologies like top-down, bottom-up, and value theory to ensure robust and defensible market figures. By leveraging this agent, you can confidently articulate the true scope of your business opportunity, validate your strategic decisions, and present compelling data to investors. It's an indispensable tool for transforming vague ideas into concrete, data-backed business plans, enabling clearer vision and more effective resource allocation.

# When to Use This Skill
- •A startup founder needs to present TAM, SAM, and SOM figures in a pitch deck for potential investors.
- •A product manager is evaluating the potential market for a new feature or product line extension.
- •A business analyst is conducting due diligence for an acquisition target and needs to validate their market claims.
- •An entrepreneur is exploring a new business idea and needs to understand its overall market viability and potential.

# Pro Tips
- 💡Always combine at least two methodologies (e.g., top-down and bottom-up) to cross-validate your market sizing results and improve accuracy.
- 💡When defining SAM, be realistic about your initial target segments and geographic reach. Overestimating can lead to flawed strategy.
- 💡Clearly articulate your assumptions when presenting market sizes, as these are critical for stakeholders to understand the basis of your calculations.
