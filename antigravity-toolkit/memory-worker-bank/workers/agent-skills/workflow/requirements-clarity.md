Navigating the initial phases of software development often involves grappling with nebulous requirements. This AI Agent Skill acts as your diligent co-pilot, meticulously dissecting ambiguity to forge crystal-clear directives. By employing a structured dialogue and applying principles like YAGNI and KISS, it ensures every line of code you write is purposeful and aligned with well-defined project goals. Prevent costly refactors and missed deadlines by establishing an unshakable foundation of understanding, transforming 'what if' into 'what is' before a single commit.

# When to Use This Skill
- •Initiating a new feature or project based on a vague request like 'implement payments' or 'create a dashboard' without specified technology or constraints.
- •Before starting work on a complex feature estimated to take more than two days, especially when involving multiple components or integrations.
- •Coordinating development efforts across different teams where misinterpretations of requirements could lead to significant rework or misalignment.
- •When reviewing a product requirement document (PRD) or user story that lacks clear acceptance criteria, edge cases, or error handling details.

# Pro Tips
- 💡Always provide the initial vague requirement as naturally as possible; the skill is designed to parse and clarify, not invent.
- 💡Be ready to engage in a focused dialogue. The skill's effectiveness heavily relies on your responsiveness to its clarifying questions.
- 💡Actively use the 'Why?' (YAGNI) and 'Simpler?' (KISS) prompts suggested by the skill to proactively refine scope and avoid over-engineering.
